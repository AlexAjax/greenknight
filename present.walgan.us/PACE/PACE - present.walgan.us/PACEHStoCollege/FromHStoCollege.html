From High School to College

Step 1: Movement into Independence

Students are moving into an adult-to-adult and adult-to-community relationships, conversations and responsibilities.

Students bring something to the table and ask others for feedback.

Students become the voice of the conversation instead of the listener.

Students will engage conversations with topics of interests and ideas

Faculty, advisers, staff will be the listener until they role change into adviser, mentor or administrator.

Step 2: Selecting Courses

Selection of courses will depend on requirements, interests, desires and new interest.

Why am I selecting this course?

    It is a GER requirement

    It is a major requirement

    It is a minor requirement

    It is an elective that fulfills a requirement

    It something interesting and new

    It fits my schedule.

Selection of courses should include courses that reflect student's aims, desires, interests, and requirements.

Reading course descriptions, researching professors and talking to other classmates will help students determine if they are meeting their interests, goals and requirements.

Sometimes courses that fit the schedule bring an unexpected new interest but something it could bring dissatisfaction.

Students have the final word on their academic schedules and success.

Step 3: Asking for Help

Communicating with members of the community such as student leaders, SAs, RAs, upperclassmen, department staff or professors, class dean, OUE academic advisers and the PACE Program staff will help students sort out their personal affairs, academic interest, classroom concerns, health and illnesses and balancing one's life.

Needing help comes at awkward times when students never needed help before. Help is not a sign of weakness but only a condition of necessity when it comes to moving forward.

Communicating and engaging with others will be major components to a students' success.

Step 4: Time Management

Time management is a key factor for students balancing and managing their lives. There is a time for everything.

What things must a student manage?

  • Sleep-sleep must be maintained in order to make good decision. Lack of sleep could cause a long domino effect of bad decisions.

  • Prioritize-students must organize their daily routines in order manage their time.

  • Social Necessities-Participate in social and club events for enjoyment but not to the point of being overwhelmed.

  • Use resources: Offices and classes such as EPASS, OUE academic advisers, Health 101, and PACE 101 can give instructions and advice on how to manage time.

  • The college and campus resources are major components of students' success in college.
